Spiral Jetty (1970)
-------------------

![Spiral
Jetty](https://www.theartstory.org/images20/works/american_art_9.jpg?2)

-   Robert Smithson
-   1500 foot spiral in Great Salk Lake
-   [Earth Work](https://www.moma.org/collection/terms/149) or [Earth
    Art](https://www.theartstory.org/movement/earth-art/)
    -   shaping the land itself
    -   using natural materials like rocks and tree branches
    -   ranges from temporary small alterations to long lasting,
        significant ones
    -   installations can also be displayed inside galleries

I Shop Therefore I Am (1990)
----------------------------

![I Shop Therefore I
am](https://www.theartstory.org/images20/works/american_art_11.jpg?2)

-   [Barbara Kruger](https://www.theartstory.org/artist/kruger-barbara/)
    -   best known for her prints
    -   tounge-in-cheek satire on *Reaganomics* and consumerism
    -   critique of social, cultural, political things
-   cut-outs from magazines
-   [Feminist](https://www.moma.org/collection/terms/168)
    -   challenge dominance of men and assumptions about womanhood
    -   recently also covers intersections with race, class, forms of
        privilege, gender identity, fluidity
-   [Conceptual](https://www.theartstory.org/movement/conceptual-art/)
    -   prizes ideas over the formal or visual components of art

![Your body is a
battleground](https://www.thebroad.org/sites/default/files/art/kruger_your_body.jpg)

Napoleon Leading the Army Over the Alps (2005)
----------------------------------------------

![Napoleon Leading the Army Over the
Alps](https://www.theartstory.org/images20/works/american_art_12.jpg?2)

-   [Kehinde Wiley](https://www.theartstory.org/artist/wiley-kehinde/)
    -   painted Obama's official protrait in 2017
-   oil on canvas
-   [appropriation](https://www.tate.org.uk/art/art-terms/a/appropriation)
    and re-visioning of the famous painting
    -   artists using pre-existing objects or other works of art with
        little transformation
    -   traced back to collages of cubists from 1912
