### What is action painting?

[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Action_painting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Action_painting)

- also called _gestural abstraction_
- paint is spontaneously splashed, dribbled or smeared 
- emphasized the act of painting as essential act
- closely associated with abstract impressionism
- act of creation is central, art as a process rather than a product

### What are color field paintings like?

[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Color_field](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Color_field)

- style of abstract painting that emerged in NY in the 40s and 50s
- related to abstract impressionism and inspired by European modernism
- characterized by large, flat fields of solid colors
- unbroken surface and flat picture plane
- color itself becomes the subject of the painting

### What are non-objective paintings like?

[https://www.tate.org.uk/art/art-terms/n/non-objective-art](https://www.tate.org.uk/art/art-terms/n/non-objective-art)

- type of abstract art that is usually geometric
- wants to convey a sense of simplicity and purity
- _Wassily Kandinsky_ was one of the pioneers of this artform
- inspired by Plato's belief that geometry was the highest form of beauty
- they don't show concrete objects but only geometrical shapes

### What is the meaning of Shahn's painting _The Passion of Sacco and Vanzetti_?

[https://whitney.org/collection/works/1022](https://whitney.org/collection/works/1022)

- one of 23 paintings of Ben Shahn created about the trial of two Italians
- working class immigrants Nicola Sacco and Bartolomeio Vanzetti 
- both were sentenced to death in 1927 for the armed robbery and murder of
a shoe company paymaster and his guard in Massachusetts
- conviction was based on circumstantial evidence created tons of outrage
globally -- corruption and discrimination was suspected
- death sentence was upheld by three commissioners who are seen here looking at
the two men whose deaths they gave the go ahead
- in the background the judge that presided over the case can be seen giving an
oath

### Which art does capitalism favor: Social Realism or Abstract Expressionism?

_Social Realism_

[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_realism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_realism)

- aims to draw attention to the real socio-economic conditions of the working class
- critiques the power structures behind those conditions
- reveal tensions between an oppressive, hegemonic force and its victims
- expose deteriorating conditions of the working class
- hold governments and social systems accountable
- 1930s, Great Depression Era

_Abstract Expressionism_

[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abstract_expressionism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abstract_expressionism)

- developed in New York in 1940s
- movement has image of rebellious, anarchic, nihilistic
- after WWII Social Realism was not acceptable

- abstract enough to be acceptable, not abstract enough to be apolitical
